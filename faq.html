How do I report a power outage?

Updated 2 days ago

You can report outages through our mobile app, by calling our 24/7 hotline at 1-800-POWER-ON, or via the outage reporting tool on our website. Our automated system will provide an estimated restoration time based on the nature of the outage.

Why is my bill higher than usual?

Updated 1 week ago

Higher bills can result from increased usage, rate adjustments, or estimated readings. We offer free energy audits to help identify usage patterns. Seasonal changes often account for significant variations - heating in winter and cooling in summer typically increase energy consumption by 20-30%.

What should I do if I see a downed power line?

Updated 3 days ago

Stay away and call emergency services immediately. Assume all downed lines are live and dangerous. Keep at least 30 feet away. If a power line falls on your vehicle, stay inside unless there's immediate danger like fire, then jump clear without touching the ground and vehicle simultaneously.

How often is equipment inspected?

Updated 2 weeks ago

We perform routine inspections every 3-6 months on critical infrastructure. All equipment undergoes thorough testing before installation. Transformers and substations receive thermal imaging scans annually to detect potential issues before they cause problems.

How long do outages typically last?

Updated 5 days ago

Most outages are restored within 2-4 hours. Severe weather events may take longer. Check our outage map for real-time restoration estimates. Our priority sequence is: 1) Emergency situations 2) Transmission lines 3) Substations 4) Main distribution lines 5) Neighborhood taps 6) Individual services.

What payment options are available?

Updated 1 month ago

We accept online payments, automatic bank drafts, credit/debit cards, mail-in checks, and in-person payments at authorized locations. Budget billing is available to spread annual costs evenly over 12 months. Payment extensions of up to 10 days can be requested online for qualifying accounts.

Is it safe to use generators during outages?

Updated 1 week ago

Generators can be used safely if properly installed. Always operate portable generators outdoors away from windows to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning. Never connect a generator directly to your home's wiring without a proper transfer switch - this can backfeed power into lines and endanger utility workers.

What causes flickering lights?

Updated 3 weeks ago

Brief flickering might be caused by high-demand appliances starting up. Persistent flickering could indicate loose wiring connections either in your home or on our system. If flickering affects multiple homes or lasts more than a few minutes, please report it as it may indicate equipment needing repair.

How does solar panel installation affect my service?

Updated 2 days ago

Solar installations require approval and a new net meter. When generating excess power, it flows back to the grid and you receive credits. There's a one-time interconnection fee of $100. System size is limited to 120% of your annual usage. Battery storage systems require additional safety inspections.

What are the different charges on my bill?

Updated 2 months ago

Your bill includes: 1) Energy charge (per kWh used) 2) Base service charge (fixed daily cost) 3) Distribution charge (maintaining local infrastructure) 4) State taxes and fees 5) Renewable energy fund contribution (0.5% of total). Detailed explanations are available in the "Understanding Your Bill" section of our website.

Why does power go out when it rains?

Updated 1 week ago

Rain can cause outages when water penetrates damaged equipment or when wind accompanies the rain, causing tree branches to contact lines. Our system has weatherproofing, but older components may fail when wet. We're gradually replacing vulnerable equipment with submersible designs that better resist moisture.

Do you offer net metering for solar customers?

Updated 3 days ago

Yes, we offer 1:1 net metering where excess generation credits your account at the full retail rate. Credits roll over month-to-month with an annual "true-up" where remaining credits are paid at the wholesale rate (currently $0.03/kWh). Systems must be sized to not exceed 120% of your annual consumption.
